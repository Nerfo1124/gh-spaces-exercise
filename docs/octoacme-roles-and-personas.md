# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designers

### Role Summary
UX Designers shape user flows and interfaces so solutions are useful, usable, and aligned to customer needs.

### Responsibilities
- Translate product requirements into user journeys, wireframes, and interaction patterns
- Run lightweight user research and usability validation before and during delivery
- Partner with Developers on implementation details, accessibility, and interaction trade-offs
- Partner with Product Managers to refine requirements and acceptance criteria for usability outcomes
- Align with Project Managers on design milestones, dependencies, and handoff readiness

### Goals
- Reduce usability issues discovered late in execution or after release
- Improve customer adoption and task completion outcomes
- Keep design decisions clear and actionable for delivery teams

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Developers and Product Managers during planning and execution
- Handoff notes and annotated mockups shared with Developers before implementation
- Milestone and dependency updates with Project Managers in delivery syncs

---

## DevOps Engineers

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers enable reliable delivery by owning CI/CD quality, deployment automation, and runtime observability.

### Responsibilities
- Maintain build, test, and deployment pipelines used by Developers and QA
- Define release guardrails, environment standards, and rollback readiness
- Partner with Developers to improve instrumentation, reliability, and operability
- Partner with Project Managers on release planning, dependency sequencing, and deployment windows
- Partner with Product Managers on rollout strategy and production readiness for customer-facing releases

### Goals
- Reduce deployment risk and time-to-restore
- Increase delivery throughput with consistent automation
- Improve system reliability and release confidence

### Typical Communication
- Release readiness check-ins with Project Managers and Product Managers
- Pipeline status, incidents, and remediation updates with Developers and QA Lead
- Post-release verification and incident follow-ups in shared team channels

---

## Solutions Architects

### Role Summary
Solutions Architects define and review technical architecture so solutions are scalable, maintainable, and aligned with business goals.

### Responsibilities
- Produce and review architecture options, constraints, and integration patterns
- Partner with Product Managers to balance customer outcomes, scope, and technical feasibility
- Partner with Developers on design decisions, non-functional requirements, and implementation risks
- Partner with Project Managers to highlight architectural dependencies and sequencing impacts
- Support QA and DevOps with quality attributes such as performance, reliability, and security expectations

### Goals
- Prevent late-stage rework caused by architecture gaps
- Ensure consistent technical direction across teams
- Enable delivery teams to implement with clear constraints and standards

### Typical Communication
- Architecture reviews with Developers, Product Managers, and QA Lead
- Trade-off and dependency decisions logged with Project Managers
- Design guidance and risk notes shared before planning commitments

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Lead defines test strategy and quality gates to ensure releases meet acceptance criteria and reliability expectations.

### Responsibilities
- Define test approach across unit, integration, and end-to-end coverage
- Partner with Developers to improve testability and defect prevention
- Partner with Product Managers to validate acceptance criteria and release readiness
- Partner with Project Managers to track quality risks, defect trends, and go/no-go criteria
- Coordinate with DevOps Engineers on CI test gating and release verification checks

### Goals
- Reduce escaped defects and production regressions
- Keep quality criteria explicit throughout planning and execution
- Improve confidence in release decisions

### Typical Communication
- Test planning and quality gate reviews with Developers and Product Managers
- Defect triage, trend reporting, and escalation updates with Project Managers
- Release readiness sign-off inputs with DevOps Engineers and delivery leads
